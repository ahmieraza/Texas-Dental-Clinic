Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our dental services and treatments

We recommend visiting the dentist every six months for routine checkups and professional cleanings. However, some patients may need more frequent visits depending on their oral health condition, gum disease risk, or specific dental needs. During your visit, our dentist will assess your individual situation and recommend the appropriate schedule for your ongoing care.

Yes, we accept most major dental insurance plans. Our team will help you understand your coverage and maximize your benefits. We also offer flexible payment options and financing plans for treatments not fully covered by insurance. Please bring your insurance card to your appointment, and we'll verify your benefits before treatment.

Your first visit will include a comprehensive oral examination, digital X-rays if needed, and a discussion of your dental history and concerns. We'll create a personalized treatment plan and explain any recommended procedures. The appointment typically takes 60-90 minutes, and we'll ensure you feel comfortable and informed throughout the process.

Professional teeth whitening is completely safe when performed by trained dental professionals. Our in-office whitening treatments can brighten your teeth by several shades in just one session. Results typically last 1-3 years, depending on your diet, lifestyle habits, and oral hygiene. We also provide take-home maintenance kits to help extend your results.

Orthodontic treatment duration varies depending on the complexity of your case, but most patients wear braces for 12-24 months. Simple alignment issues may take as little as 6-12 months, while more complex cases might require 2-3 years. During your consultation, our orthodontist will provide a personalized timeline based on your specific needs and treatment goals.

Fillings are used to repair small to moderate cavities by removing decay and filling the space with composite material. Crowns are used when a tooth is severely damaged, weakened, or has undergone root canal treatment. A crown covers the entire visible portion of the tooth, providing strength and protection. Your dentist will recommend the best option based on the extent of damage and your tooth's condition.

Dental implant surgery is performed under local anesthesia, so you won't feel pain during the procedure. Post-operative discomfort is typically mild and manageable with over-the-counter pain medication. Most patients return to normal activities within 2-3 days. The complete healing and integration process takes 3-6 months, during which the implant fuses with your jawbone for maximum stability.

For dental emergencies, call our office immediately at (713) 555-1234. For severe pain, rinse with warm salt water and take over-the-counter pain medication. If a tooth is knocked out, keep it moist and try to reinsert it gently. For broken teeth, save any pieces and rinse your mouth with warm water. We offer same-day emergency appointments and will provide immediate care to relieve pain and address urgent issues.

Maintain good oral hygiene by brushing twice daily with fluoride toothpaste, flossing daily, and using an antimicrobial mouthwash. Limit sugary and acidic foods and drinks, don't smoke or use tobacco products, and visit us regularly for professional cleanings and checkups. A balanced diet rich in calcium and vitamins also supports healthy teeth and gums.

Children should have their first dental visit by age 1 or within six months of their first tooth appearing. Early visits help establish good oral hygiene habits, allow us to monitor proper tooth development, and help children become comfortable with dental care. Our pediatric dentist specializes in making these visits positive and educational experiences for both children and parents.

Yes, we offer several sedation options to help anxious patients feel comfortable during treatment. Options include nitrous oxide (laughing gas), oral sedation, and IV sedation for more complex procedures. Our team will discuss the best option for your comfort level and treatment needs. We're committed to ensuring every patient has a relaxed and positive dental experience.

Treatment costs vary depending on the complexity and type of procedure needed. We provide detailed treatment estimates before beginning any work and will discuss all costs upfront. We accept most insurance plans and offer flexible payment options and financing to make dental care affordable. Our team will work with you to maximize your insurance benefits and find payment solutions that fit your budget.

Clear aligners are virtually invisible, removable plastic trays that gradually straighten teeth. They can be removed for eating, brushing, and special occasions. Traditional braces use metal or ceramic brackets and wires that are fixed to your teeth. Both are effective, but clear aligners offer more flexibility and aesthetics, while braces may be better for complex orthodontic cases. Our orthodontist will recommend the best option for your specific needs.

Before oral surgery, follow all pre-operative instructions provided by our team. This typically includes fasting for a specified period if sedation will be used, arranging transportation to and from the appointment, and taking any prescribed medications as directed. Wear comfortable clothing and avoid smoking or alcohol. We'll provide detailed pre and post-operative care instructions to ensure the best possible outcome.

We use state-of-the-art technology including digital X-rays, 3D CBCT imaging, intraoral cameras, laser dentistry, and digital impression systems. Our advanced sterilization equipment ensures the highest safety standards. This technology allows for more accurate diagnoses, less invasive treatments, reduced radiation exposure, and improved patient comfort. We continuously invest in the latest dental innovations to provide the best possible care.
